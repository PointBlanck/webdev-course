My Series Rankings

A short article on my top 3 favourite TV series

Big Bang Theory

Why it matters to me?

As a major nerd growing up, I had to deal with a great deal of bullying, feeling inferior and never good enough no matter how hard I was trying at school. Big Bang Theory was the first series I watched in full that managed to make me fell better about my life choices, my skills and talents and my academic achievements.

Plot

Leonard Hofstadter and Sheldon Cooper are both brilliant physicists working at Cal Tech in Pasadena, California. They are colleagues, best friends, and roommates, although in all capacities their relationship is always tested primarily by Sheldon's regimented, deeply eccentric, and non-conventional ways. They are also friends with their Cal Tech colleagues mechanical engineer Howard Wolowitz and astrophysicist Rajesh Koothrappali. The foursome spend their time working on their individual work projects, playing video games, watching science-fiction movies, or reading comic books. As they are self-professed nerds, all have little or no luck with women. When Penny, a pretty woman and an aspiring actress from Omaha, moves into the apartment across the hall from Leonard and Sheldon's, Leonard has another aspiration in life, namely to get Penny to be his girlfriend.

House M.D

Why it matters to me

This is not the kind of series that I normally go for because of the darkness under which humanity is being portrayed. However, the troubled nature of the protagonist, Dr. House, and the gradual breaking down of the characters in the series makes for an excelent character study and makes you feel like you're getting to know real people, with real problems and real vices. All that wrapped into a exceptional medical drama. An excelent series to watch for when you're feeling down.

Plot

Using a crack team of doctors and his wits, an antisocial maverick doctor specializing in diagnostic medicine does whatever it takes to solve puzzling cases that come his way.

Silicon Valley

Why it matters to me

If you're an individual with heaps of unjustified confidence in their own ability to do things better than anybody else, then Silicon Valley will satisfy that itch you get to make your own company and make yourself rich off of your genius self. Silicon Valley is the story of a bunch of friends/roomates coming up with an revolutionary new idea and trying to launch their start-up company against incredible odds and having to fight tech giants that want to buy them out, competitors wanting to steal their idea and enemies trying to sue them for their own piece of the pie. It's an incredible underdog story portrayed as the messy, comical and all-over-the-place drama that starting your own company when you have no idea what you're doing would be.

Plot

Follows the struggle of Richard Hendricks, a Silicon Valley engineer trying to build his own company called Pied Piper.
